Alfa Romeo came into Formula 1 in 1950, competing and suppling engines sporadically between 1950 and 1987. After winning drivers championships in 1950 and 1951, they withdrew from the sport. They returned in 1979 but left again in 1985. The Alfa Romeo logo returned to Formula One in 2015, appearing on the Scuderia Ferrari cars and in 2017, Alfa Romeo announced that they were to become title sponsors for Sauber from 2018, and had entered into a technical and commercial partnership with the team and returned to the sport when Sauber was rebranded in 2019. Alfa Romeo's drivers for the 2023 season are Valtteri Bottas and Zhou Guanyu.

Renault's Formula 1 team was renamed Alpine for 2021 season. They came into Formula 1 in 1981 by the name of Toleman, with 3 podeum finishes in 1984. Toleman was then taken over by fashion brand Bennetton in 1986, who achieved 102 podiums in their 15 years, before being rebranded to Renault in 2002. Renault sold shares to Genii Capital in 2010 and signed a deal with Loftus in 2011 for the team to become Loftus Renault/ Loftus. Genii Capital sold the team back to Renault in 2015, with the return of Renault in Formula 1 in 2016 and podiums in 2020, before rebranding to Alpine. Alpine's drivers for the 2023 season are Pierre Gasly and Esteban Ocon.

Aston Martin first entered Formula 1 in 1959, failing to score any points in that year and also 1960. This resulted in them leaving Formula 1 after 1960. In 2007, Vijay Mallya and Michiel Mol purchased Spyker F1, also known as Etihad Aldar Spyker F1 Team, and rebranded it to Force India. Lawrence Stroll purchased Force India in 2018 after it went into administration as Vijay Mallya faced legal and financial issues and rebranded it to Racing Point, finishing fourth in the Constructures Championship in 2020. In 2020, Stroll purchased 16.7% of Aston Martin, and rebranded to it in 2021. Aston Martin's drivers for the 2023 season are Lance Stroll and Fernando Alonso.

The racing branch of Ferrari was created by Enzo Ferrari, an entrepreneur and race driver. Ferrari first entered Formula One in 1950, entering the Monaco Grand Prix that year. Ferrari are the most successful racing team in Formula One history. Drivers have won 15 championships, with Ferrari themselves winning 16 constructors’ championships Ferrari have claimed 239 race victories out of 1032 race entries, with drivers reaching the podium in 778 of those races. Ferarri’s current drivers for the 2023 season are Charles Leclerc and Carlos Sainz.

Gene Haas, a NASCAR team owner annocuned that Haas was entering Formula 1 in 2014. They finished eighth in the Constructures Championship in their debut year in 2016 and again in 2017. Haas' drivers for the 2023 season are Kevin Magnussen and Nico Hulkenberg

Bruce McLaren founded Bruce McLaren Motor Racing in 1963. The team made it's debut in Monaco in 1966, with their first win coming in the Belgian Grand Prix in 1968. Mika Häkkinen delivered back-to-back titles in 1998 and 1999 although the 1998 constructors’ title remains its most recent success. They finished third in the Championship in 2020. McLaren's drivers for the 2023 season are Lando Norris and Oscar Piastri.

Mercedes first entered Formula 1 in 1954 but withdrew in 1955 after the Le Mans disaster. They returned in 1994 and won one constructors' title and three drivers' titles in a works partnership with McLaren which lasted until 2009. Mercedes has become one of the most successful teams in Formula One history, winning seven consecutive Drivers' titles from 2014 to 2020 and eight consecutive Constructors' titles from 2014 to 2021. It has set records for most wins in a season (19 in 2016), and for most consecutive constructors' titles. Mercedes' drivers for the 2023 season are Lewis Hamilton and George Russell.

Red Bull had been invloved in Formula 1 from 1995 through sponsership, but didn't begin racing until 2005. They won four successive Drivers' and Constructors' Championship titles from 2010 to 2013, becoming the first Austrian team to win the title, as well as winning the Drivers' Championship in 2021 and 2022 as well as the Constructors Championship. Red Bull's drivers for 2023 are Max Verstappen and Sergio Perez.

Scuderia AlphaTauri originally began as Scuderia Toro Rosso in 2006 and is Red Bull's sister team. The team's first podium and only pole position and victory were scored by Sebastian Vettel at the 2008 Italian Grand Prix. They rebranded to Scuderia AlphaTauri in 2020 promote AlphaTauri's fashion brand. Since then they have had 2 podiums and one win. AlphaTauri's drivers for the 2023 season are Nyck De Vris and Yuki Tsunoda.

Williams entered Formula 1 in 1977 and won their first race in 1979. They won nine Constructors' Championships between 1980 and 1997. A decline in results during 2016 and 2017 turned into a crisis in 2018, when the team scored just seven points and came last in the constructors’ championship and were unable to score a single point, finishing the year last in the constructors’ title for the third season running in 2020. Williams' drivers for 2023 are Alex Albon and Logan Sargeant.
