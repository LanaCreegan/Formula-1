What about teams?

Alfa Romeo came into Formula 1 in 1950, competing sporadically between 1950 and 1987. The Alfa Romeo logo returned to Formula One in 2015, and in 2017, they announced that they were to become sponsors for Sauber, returning to the sport when Sauber was rebranded in 2019. Alfa Romeo's drivers for the 2023 season are Valtteri Bottas and Zhou Guanyu.

Renault's Formula 1 team was renamed Alpine for 2021 season, entering Formula 1 in 1981 originally as Toleman. Renault has won two constructors' and drivers' championships, while Alpine has 1 win and 2 podiums. Alpine's drivers for the 2023 season are Pierre Gasly and Esteban Ocon.

Aston Martin first entered Formula 1 in 1959, and left after 1960. In 2007, Spyker F1 was purchased, and rebranded to Force India. Force India was rebranded to Racing Point in 2018, finishing fourth in the Constructures Championship in 2020. In 2021 it rebranded to Aston Martin. Aston Martin's drivers for the 2023 season are Lance Stroll and Fernando Alonso.

Ferrari entered Formula One in 1950, entering the Monaco Grand Prix that year and is one of the most successful racing team in Formula One history. Drivers have won 15 championships and 16 constructors’ championships. Ferrari’s current driver’s for the 2023 season are Charles Leclerc and Carlos Sainz.

Gene Haas, a NASCAR team owner annocuned that Haas was entering Formula 1 in 2014. They finished eighth in the Constructures Championship in their debut year in 2016 and again in 2017. Haas' drivers for the 2023 season are Kevin Magnussen and Nico Hulkenberg

McLaren made it's debut in Monaco in 1966, with their first win coming in the Belgian Grand Prix in 1968. They finished third in the Championship in 2020. McLaren's drivers for the 2023 season are Lando Norris and Oscar Piastri.

Mercedes first entered Formula 1 in 1954 and has become one of the most successful teams in Formula One history, winning seven consecutive Drivers' titles from 2014 to 2020 and eight consecutive Constructors' titles from 2014 to 2021. Mercedes' drivers for the 2023 season are Lewis Hamilton and George Russell.

Red Bull began racing in Formula One in 2005. They won four successive Drivers' and Constructors' Championship titles from 2010 to 2013, and again in 2021 and 2022 as well as the Constructors Championship. Red Bull's drivers for 2023 are Max Verstappen and Sergio Perez.

Scuderia AlphaTauri originally began as Scuderia Toro Rosso in 2006 and are Red Bull’s sister team. They rebranded to Scuderia AlphaTauri in 2020, since then they have only had 2 podiums and one win. AlphaTauri's drivers for the 2023 season are Nyck De Vris and Yuki Tsunoda.

Williams entered Formula One in 1977. Results declined from 2016 to 2018, scoring just seven points and came last in the constructors’ championship. They were unable to score a single point, finishing the year last in the constructors’ title for the third season running in 2020. Williams' drivers for 2023 are Alex Albon and Logan Sargeant.
